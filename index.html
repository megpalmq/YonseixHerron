About the Collaboration

Yonsei University and Herron School of Art + Design students unite to create impactful solutions addressing food scarcity and health challenges for elderly communities in Korea and Indianapolis. In this project, each team was assigned a distinct topic, with the goal of presenting the information in a creative and engaging way. To ensure variety and uniqueness, each team was assigned a different style of displaying their data, whether it be through an infographic poster or an infographic brochure. These formats allowed us to tailor our design to the nature of the topic, using visuals, typography, and layout to communicate the message effectively. By diversifying the presentation styles, we were able to showcase a wide range of design techniques and visual storytelling methods, each aligned with the specific needs and tone of the assigned topic.

The teams consitent of students from both universities, where they each assigned roles that included: project leader, designers, researchers, and project cordinator. Each team member was responsible for their assigned role, and they worked together to create a cohesive and impactful project. The project was a great opportunity for students to learn from each other and gain valuable experience in collaboration and project management.
